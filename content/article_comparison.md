## 1. Persistence vs. Amnesia

**ChatGPT:** "Hi, who are you again?"
Every new chat is a blank slate. You have to paste your context, your rules, your business model every single time.

**Clawdbot:** "Welcome back, Oscar. I monitored your competitors while you slept. The price of BTC is $82k, so I didn't fire the sniper bot. Also, here is the invoice you asked for yesterday."
It remembers. It has a file system. It has a memory. It lives on your server 24/7.

## 2. Sandbox vs. Reality

**ChatGPT:** Can analyze a CSV file you upload.
**Clawdbot:** Can SSH into your server, deploy a Docker container, update your Stripe webhook, and push code to GitHub.

ChatGPT lives in a browser tab.
Clawdbot lives in the **Real World**. It has keys to your infrastructure. It can touch money (Stripe), code (GitHub), and customers (Gmail/Twilio).

## 3. Passive vs. Proactive

**ChatGPT:** Waits for you to type. If you don't type, it does nothing.
**Clawdbot:** Text you first.
"Hey, your server CPU is high."
"Hey, a new lead just paid $149."
"Hey, I found a viral tweet you should reply to."

It works while you sleep. It creates value without your input.

## 4. Renting vs. Owning

**ChatGPT:** You are a user. OpenAI owns the model, the data, and the rules. They can ban you or censor you.
**Clawdbot:** You are the owner. You run it on your own DigitalOcean droplet. You own the logs. You pick the model (Claude, GPT-4, Llama 3). No one can turn it off.
